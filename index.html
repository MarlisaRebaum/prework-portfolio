Hello,

so lovely to meet you!

You know that little emoji with the head exploding and brain popping out of it? That describes me right now. As you are reading this, I most likely look exactly like that while staring at my computer while simultaneously stress-baking a cake that, if eaten in its entirety, will make all of my stress vanish in the form of a food-coma later this evening. I have taken on a new challenge of becoming a web developer and although I am so grateful and excited for the opportunity, it is unlike anything I have ever done.

During high school, when I first considered the possibility of even going to college, I felt that as a woman, technical fields were never presented as an option from my career advisors. I ended up earning both a bachelor's and master's degree in teaching. Through my extensive college experience, I learned that although I enjoy being a teacher in terms of helping others learn new materials, supporting others in their learning goals, and using creative problem solving to make the classroom and learning run more smoothly, I do not so much enjoy teaching in a traditional classroom. As I have grown more confident in my skills and abilities through my education, I have also gained the confidence to go outside my comfort zone and explore other career opportunities that align more with my long-term dreams and passions.

I want to be a software developer because it allows me to use a combination of creative thinking and problem solving. As I am a firm believer of lifelong learning, I am excited for a career that allows many opportunities for professional and personal growth. I know this journey is going to be difficult, but I am excited to start on an exciting, new careers path.
